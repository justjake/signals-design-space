# Bun/JSC vs Node/V8: do the rankings survive an engine change?

2026-07-04. All numbers measured fresh this session, node and bun runs
interleaved back-to-back on the same machine.

**Headline: yes, the rankings change — in both directions.** The two
typed-array designs swap fates: **arena (full Int32Array core) goes from
V8's loser to JSC's winner**, and **arrayd (inline-first edge arrays) goes
from V8-competitive to JSC's clear loser** (kairo/repeatedObservers 3.2x
vs alien). alien-v3 loses its quiet-read crown under JSC and regresses
*absolutely* on kairo/unstable. Meanwhile most workloads are simply much
faster under bun (kairo/avoidablePropagation 97 ms → 42 ms), and the
`create` shape collapses from ~5 ms + heavy GC to ~0.9 ms.

## Environment

- Apple M4 Max, macOS (Darwin 25.5.0), ambient loadavg 3.3–4.2 throughout
  (recorded per result file). Ratios within a runtime are the reliable
  signal; node/bun pairs were run back-to-back for fair absolute compares.
- Node v24.16.0 (V8). Bun 1.3.14+0d9b296af (JavaScriptCore).
- Frameworks: alien-v3 (upstream v3.2.1 baseline), control, sweep, arrayd,
  arena, arena-links. All six load and run cleanly under bun.

## Tooling changes (this session)

- `harness/bench/shapes.ts`: new `--runtime node|bun` flag. Parent spawns
  children as `bun <shapes.ts>` (bun executes TS directly) instead of
  `pnpm exec tsx`. JSC exposes no `PerformanceObserver('gc')` entry type
  (checked via `supportedEntryTypes`), so children report `gcN: -1` and
  the table prints **gc:n/a** under bun instead of a misleading gc:0.
- `harness/bench/run.ts`: new `--runtime node|bun` flag with a local
  `runBunChild` spawner (util/cli.ts left untouched). The esbuild-bundled
  child runs under bun **with `--expose-gc` kept**: bun supports the flag
  and installs a `globalThis.gc` backed by JSC's synchronous collector, so
  the milomg suites' between-run `gc()` calls work in both runtimes.
  Bun results files get a `-bun` suffix and a `runtime` metadata field.
- Node paths are unchanged and re-verified (default `--runtime node`
  goes through the exact same `util/cli.ts runChild` code as before).

## Correctness under JSC

- **Cross-runtime checksums: all match.** Every shapes child was run under
  node and bun for all six frameworks; all 8 shape checksums are identical
  per framework across runtimes (48/48 match). No within-run cross-framework
  mismatches in any pass either.
- **Asserting suites pass under bun**: `cellx` (asserts exact cell values)
  and `dynamic` (asserts exact computed pull counts, `testPullCounts: true`)
  ran for alien-v3/arrayd/arena under bun with zero `console.assert`
  failures, exit 0. No wrong-result findings under JSC.

## Ranking inversions (the deliverable)

1. **arena: V8 laggard → JSC front-runner.** Under node, arena loses kairo
   badly where it matters (avoidable 1.19x, repeatedObservers 1.32x,
   unstable 1.40x vs alien). Under bun it *wins outright*: mux 0.76x,
   triangle 0.74x, unstable 0.84x, broad 0.92x, deep 0.95x, ties the rest;
   and it crushes the dynamic suite (6-10x10 at **0.55x** vs alien, vs
   0.79x under node). In shapes, its reads/isolate advantage doubles
   (reads 0.94–0.97x → 0.50–0.80x; isolate 0.88–0.91x → 0.49–0.67x).
2. **arrayd: V8-competitive → JSC's worst.** Under node arrayd ties or
   beats alien on most of kairo (deep 0.93x, unstable 0.82x, repeated
   0.95x). Under bun it is last almost everywhere: **repeatedObservers
   3.2x**, diamond 1.42x, unstable 1.37x, broad 1.22–1.29x, triangle
   1.25x, dyn 3-5x500 1.33x. Fully reproduced across two bun runs.
3. **create: arena's V8 win inverts, and the whole shape collapses.**
   node: alien ~5.5 ms with 17–38 ms GC per window, arena wins at 0.9x.
   bun: alien ~0.85 ms (**~6x faster absolute**) and the typed-array
   libraries flip to losers (arena 1.33–1.49x, arrayd 1.56–1.67x,
   arena-links 1.68–2.01x).
4. **reads: alien falls from front-pack (V8) to last (JSC).** node reads
   cluster within 0.92–1.17x of alien; bun reads see arena 0.50x, arrayd
   0.56–0.92x, sweep 0.61–0.70x. alien's bun reads are also bimodal
   across passes (5.5 / 8.3 / 8.1 ms) — JSC tiering variance on its read
   loop — while arena is rock-stable at 4.0–4.4 ms.
5. **kairo/unstable: absolute regression for alien under JSC** (12.4 →
   20.4 ms, 1.65x slower than under V8) while arena holds flat (17.3 →
   17.1), flipping the winner.
6. **write: alien fastest under V8, slowest under JSC** (control 0.82–0.85x,
   sweep 0.75–0.82x under bun). Mild but consistent.
7. **sweep looks better under JSC** on pull-ish shapes (diamond 0.73–0.78x,
   broad 0.80–0.82x, dynamic 0.67–0.70x vs ~parity under V8), though its
   structural isolate weakness remains in both engines (its worst shape).
8. **Not inverted:** arena-links stays last-or-near-last in both engines;
   molBench/avoidable/deep are near-ties for everyone under bun.

## Mechanism hypotheses

- **arena up, alien down under JSC.** Our V8 wins for alien-v3/control came
  from hand-fitting hot functions under TurboFan's ~460-bytecode inline
  budget, closure-const folding, and hidden-class monomorphism — none of
  which JSC's DFG/FTL rewards the same way (different inlining cost model,
  different IC machinery), so alien's pointer-chasing linked-list walks pay
  full polymorphic get-by-id cost per hop. The arena's hot loops are
  `M[id + FIELD]` int32 loads off one Int32Array: FTL/B3 lowers these to
  bounds-checked base+offset machine loads with no shape checks at all, and
  JSC's typed-array fast paths are excellent. So the arena's constant
  factor transfers across engines while alien's V8-specific tuning
  evaporates — visible as arena's stable 4.0 ms reads vs alien's bimodal
  5.5–8.3 ms (tier-up lottery on the object-graph read loop).
- **arrayd down under JSC (repeatedObservers 3.2x).** arrayd's edge storage
  is deliberately two-shaped at every access site: inline `dep0`/`sub0`
  field *or* `deps[]`/`subs[]` array spill, plus hole-writes (undefined)
  and tombstone counters in the subs arrays. V8 handles this with
  well-understood elements-kind transitions (the design was tuned against
  them); in JSC each such site becomes a mixed get-by-id/get-by-val
  polymorphic IC over butterfly-backed arrays that hold
  objects-or-undefined, and the tombstone scan/compaction loop —
  exactly what repeatedObservers hammers via subscriber churn — appears
  not to tier into a tight loop the way V8 compiles it. The result is a
  design whose entire premise (V8 elements-kind discipline) does not
  travel: it keeps alien semantics but loses its speed rationale.
- **create 6x faster and inverted.** Under V8 this shape is GC-bound
  (17–60 ms of pauses per 12–20 reps — more GC than compute), and arena
  won by *not allocating*. JSC's Riptide GC plus bun's larger default
  nursery apparently absorbs the 10k-node build/teardown without a
  collection inside the ~1 ms window (unobservable directly — no 'gc'
  entries under bun — but implied by the 6x collapse). With GC effectively
  free, the arena's bump-pointer/free-list bookkeeping and side-array
  writes become pure overhead, flipping create to the plain-object
  designs. Caveat: min-of-reps under bun hides whatever deferred GC cost
  materializes later; the memory harness under bun would be needed to see
  the true steady-state cost.

## Absolute-speed notes

- Bun child startup: shapes children finish in ~0.5–0.6 s under bun vs
  ~0.9–1.1 s via pnpm+tsx+node (toolchain difference, not pure engine
  startup: bun runs the TS directly).
- JSC is dramatically faster on most kairo cases (avoidable 0.43x,
  diamond 0.57x, mol 0.65x, mux 0.69x) and on allocation-heavy create
  (~6x), roughly at parity on broad/deep, and *slower* on kairo/unstable
  for the object-graph libraries (alien 1.65x worse). Anyone shipping to
  Safari/Bun should not assume V8 rankings transfer — for our libraries
  they mostly don't.
- GC observability gap: PerformanceObserver('gc') does not exist in
  bun/JSC (`supportedEntryTypes = [mark, measure, resource]`), so bun-side
  GC pressure is invisible and min-time reporting flatters
  allocation-heavy designs *more* under bun. Treat bun create/write wins
  for object-graph designs with that caveat.
